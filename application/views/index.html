Dear Team,

In my 2018 end of year message to you, I reflected on the importance of strengthening internal communication and how I can better support you to realize the values, mission, and vision of UNICEF Uganda. Quite often, I find myself engaged in work that leaves me little opportunity for the kinds of face-to-face interactions that help build trusting, collaborative work communities. Starting this month, I’d like to experiment with some different approaches.

The first approach involves my introduction of a monthly bulletin
... from the Rep.
Through this bulletin, and with invaluable support from Catherine N. as well as Elizabeth, I will be able to share highlights of meetings I attend in and out of the country and any materials relevant to the work we are doing in Uganda. Beyond that, I will also be celebrating and acknowledging some of the creative projects that we support and implement within the country office. Most days, I tend to interact largely with section chiefs and senior management. I plan to use this bulletin as a chance to meet with different project teams and departments outside management to learn more of the details of what you’re working on, and what your challenges and successes are. My goal is to highlight a staff team each month.

In addition, if the need arises, I will also use this space to offer my perspective on officerelated issues that may be percolating among fellow staff-members. Organizations like UNICEF are complex and sometimes difficult to navigate. During my own career, I’ve certainly seen instances in which the organization has been slow to respond to staff concerns in the time and manner that we would wish. My hope is that this bulletin will become a way to demystify some of those processes or concerns as they arise, while allowing me the opportunity to think about what I’m doing as the Rep, and how I can improve.

For this inaugural issue of the bulletin, I would like to share some of the presentations that were highlighted at the Regional Management Team meeting in Nairobi a few weeks ago. In particular, our WASH regional advisor, Dr. Samuel Godfrey, gave an excellent presentation on regional WASH statistics. Among other things, he showed how Uganda compares to the other ESAR countries concerning the WASH SDGs 6.1 and 6.2. (We aren’t the worst, but we aren’t the best, either.)
